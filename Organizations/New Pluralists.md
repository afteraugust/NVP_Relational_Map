## About New Pluralists

New Pluralists is a cross-ideological funder collaborative founded in 2021 and headquartered virtually, investing together in a culture of respect, belonging, and pluralism across the United States. 
Founding funders include [[Einhorn Collaborative]], Stand Together Trust, , Klarman Family Foundation, Lubetzky Family Foundation, John Templeton Foundation, Acton Family Giving, and the William & Flora Hewlett Foundation, and the collaborative is working to raise and invest $100M to catalyze a cultural shift away from polarization and toward cooperation across lines of difference. Their strategy combines grantmaking, convening, and storytelling — notable investments include the $10M _Healing Starts Here_ initiative supporting 32 place-based grantees, a network of Field Builders, and the newly launched 2026 Breakthrough Fund focused on community solutions that bridge class lines. Under their 2030 Strategy, they're focused on accelerating pluralism practices, strengthening ecosystem alignment, and deepening funder engagement — highly relevant to NVP given the overlap between their "culture of belonging" frame and NVP's steward-transformation-to-culture-shift theory of change.

**Quick note for NVP strategy:** Lauren Higgins (ecosystem strategies) is likely the most relevant person for NVP's work given her explicit focus on network weaving among field builders and grantees — she'd be the natural door into their ecosystem map. Alison Grubbs (culture strategies, with _Healing Starts Here_ in her portfolio) is the second most natural fit given NVP's place-based community-healing model.
