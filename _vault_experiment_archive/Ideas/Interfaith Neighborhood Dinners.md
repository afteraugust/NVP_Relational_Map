## The Idea
Monthly neighborhood dinners hosted at different faith community venues. Not religious events — secular community meals that happen to be hosted by faith communities who have the space and the hospitality culture. Rev. Whitfield would convene his interfaith network to rotate hosting.

## Why It Matters
- **Access:** Faith communities have the best underutilized community spaces in most neighborhoods
- **Cross-community:** Dinners bring together people who wouldn't normally mix (different congregations, non-churchgoers, etc.)
- **Low cost:** Faith communities often absorb hosting costs as part of their mission
- **Trust building:** Shared meals are the oldest community-building technology
- **Data:** Natural setting for informal [[Know Your Neighbor Survey]] conversations

## Who's Involved

- Other faith leaders (not yet in our network — Rev. Whitfield is the bridge)
